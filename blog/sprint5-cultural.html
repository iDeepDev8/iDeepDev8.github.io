Reflection Foundations core skill

I have read through the past reflections on each sprint learnings and have found three new things about myself. I would first read the core learning part of the sprint and get the overall idea of what core skills I will have to try and acquire in each sprint.

The activity in core skills requires you to acquire a certain mindset that prepares you to program or think like a developer. For example, it asks you to think and write about the difference between EQ and IQ and how important each of them are.



It was a new experience for me as until this day I have not really thought much about the topics in core skills section. It was like trying to think about the things that you have never been interested in.

To be honest, first time I started this foundation course, when I saw the core skills where you had to download this app called headspace, do some mediatiting, write a reflection on it, I thought, "maybe they don't really teach coding, and just teach you to think clearly?".



But after doing reflections in core skills, trying meditating, doing research on how my brain works, and what helps me learn more efficiently I have come to realise that it is more helpful than I thought it would be. I now think that it sets up your mindset and study coding or anything in your life. These skills have helped me get less frustrated, as I started telling myself that I can pick this up whenever I get stuck on a challenge and reflect on what I thought during the last sprint and activity.

Core skills have helped me in many aspects, is changing the way I do things and improving it could improve me to become a skilled developer.
